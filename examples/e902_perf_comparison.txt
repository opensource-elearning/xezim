----------------------------------------------------------------
  Observations
----------------------------------------------------------------

  - iverilog is leanest in memory by ~7× and wins the short-run
    hello test. Its event scheduler is interpretive bytecode, which
    has lower constant overhead but scales linearly with simulation
    length.

  - xezim crosses iverilog around the memcpy size (~24K cycles) and
    pulls ahead by cmark scale, ending ~11% faster than iverilog on
    a 512K-cycle run. xezim's per-cycle cost is roughly flat (~0.7-
    0.9 ms/cycle) while iverilog grows.

  - xxxyyy's commercial event scheduler dominates at scale: ~10×
    faster than xezim on cmark. That is the gap remaining for future
    xezim optimization work (NBA scheduler, edge dispatch hot-paths,
    bytecode -> cranelift JIT for hot blocks per JIT path B memory).

  - Memory: xezim is constant ~340 MB regardless of test (per-signal
    AoS storage dominates, scales with elaborated signal count, not
    sim length). iverilog is constant ~52 MB. SoA roadmap (memory
    note xezim_soa_roadmap) would close this.
